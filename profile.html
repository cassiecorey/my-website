Music

In the 1st grade, my mother decided I should learn how to play an instrument. She gave me three choices: piano, trumbone, or violin.

When I replied, "Violin." Her response was a sigh. "You don't even know what a violin is."

"Yes I do," I said. "It has four strings and you play it like this." I lifted my hands up into the classic violin pose and pretended to play. She nodded her surpised agreement and enrolled me in Suzuki lessons.

Sports

My sports career began in highschool after a friend of mine suggested I try out for the crew team. I rowed for three years after that moving quickly up through the boats. After taking first place as 2nd seat at the Royal Henley Women's Regatta in England, I decided to focus my attention on Wrestling instead.

I had begun wrestling when the boarding school I went to required all non-freshman to be on a sports team. After trying out and being cut from the indoor track team in the winter of my sophomore year, I looked up to the elevated track above the dirt track where I had just proven my lack of running skills. Above me, clad in matching grey sweats, was a group of boys running laps in silence. Influenced by a friend of ours who was already a member of the team, my girlfriend and I decided to try out for wrestling.
